Jean Tannen and his aliases

Jean Tannen

Jean Tannen is a young thief from Camorr and a member of the Gentlemen Bastards. He is the companion and best friend of Locke Lamora. Before becoming Father Chains’ pezon Jean lived an honest, comfortable life in North Corner as an only child, alongside his merchant parents. This part of his life came to an abrupt and tragic end at the age of ten, when the shop burnt to the ground, taking his family and former life with it. Jean then became an initiate of the Nameless Thirteenth, learnt weaponry with Don Maranzalla and became affiliated into Aza Guilla’s priesthood. Despite their turbulent start, Jean and Locke forged an unlikely friendship, whereupon Jean became utterly devoted to his garrista.












Tavrin Callas

Tavrin Callas was an alias used in an elaborate plan to rob two nobles, Don Salvara and Doña Salvara, of their fortune through a plan to ship Austershalin Brandy to them through illicit galleons. Of course, the money the Salvaras paid "Lukas Fehrwight" actually went straight to the Elderglass cellar where Jean and his associates lived.
















Jerome de Ferra

Locke devices a plan to rob the wealthiest man in Tel Verrar, namely the master of the famous gambling house The Sinspire, Requin. The preparations for the game takes two entire years during which Locke and Jean, his assosiate, cement their false identities of Leocanto Kosta and Jerome de Ferra.















Jerome Valora

Jerome Valora was an alias given to him by bondsmagi, a group of magicans, in which he was instructed with the task of winning a political campaign. The alias already had a bank account and a good reputation as political consultants. Additionally, the group he was assosated with, had been "adjusted" to make their power more or less absolute.
